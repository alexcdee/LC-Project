Evaluation

I have made a few noticeable changes compared to my design stage. Initially, I started with a step tracker that would track and log the end-user's steps. The end-user is a person who is interested in monitoring their daily physical activity levels or maintaining a healthy lifestyle by keeping track of their steps using a step tracker. I also had the ability to show the time gone by and display the amount of steps taken when the end-user presses button A and button B. However, my design has undergone a few modifications. I removed some parts and implemented new ideas for the step tracker. I modified button A and button B so that the end-user can set logging to true and false when they are ready to begin their activity. Additionally, I added two more analogue inputs, a light sensor and a heat sensor, to take into account the end-user's wellbeing while outside.

While making my design, I did run into some issues. When the steps were being logged, it would log as a new step taken and go up by 1. After testing around, I found out how to change it so that the data would add 1 to the previous value so it would go like this 1, 2, ... 505. I also ran into a few issues with my Python code. While making my graph, I did struggle but I was able to overcome this with resources online. Another issue I ran into was finding the mean of my steps. It would add all the values together, giving a mean of 4,297. I was able to fix this by getting the max value of steps and dividing it by the max value of (time in seconds/60), giving me the value of 120, which was correct.

Overall, I stuck to my design of a step tracker but did make a few changes. I believe I did stick to my brief, my initial objectives of making a functional step tracker to track the end-user's physical and mental wellbeing during their activity, storing the data in a CSV file, performing statistical analysis on that CSV data and writing it in Python, and addressing my two 'what if' questions. I feel like I have achieved my goals of the basic and advanced requirements. Any issues I faced during my project, I was able to effectively fix or work around them.

  • One improvement I could do is an improvement for the end-user. I only made my data to work on if the end-user walks, but not if they decide to jog or run.
  • Another improvement I could make in the future is looking at all my goals and planning and designing all of them before starting to program. This will prevent me from running into errors and effectively saving me time during these 10 weeks.
