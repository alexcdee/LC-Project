Investigation

For my investigation I looked into various different types of ideas that can enhance some aspect of wellbeing. Wellbeing is a positive state that is experience by individuals and societies. I looked at:

  • Mental health
  • Physical health
  • Both physical and mental health

I then looked into the factors that support wellbeing:

  • Sleep
  • Money
  • Sports
  • Music
  • Meditation

I used search engines such as Microsoft Bing and Google to find out more info on wellbeing and what ideas that are implemented with an embedded system. I found out embedded systems such as the Apple Watch, Fitbit and Garmin have any analogue/digital inputs/outputs that support wellbeing such as a step tracker, heart monitor, mindfulness features, sleep tracker, movement tracker and more.

At my initial plan I wanted to do a movement tracker, where my embedded system of choice the Micro: BIT. This would track the user's activity profile every hour if they were to sit down, e.g. If they sit on the chair for a few hours, it would remind the user every hour to get up and some sort of exercise whether it would be jumping jacks or squats. But I thought that would have been too complicated for this project and would take too much time to program and analyse.

I also carried out a survey forum for people to fill out. I had questions that included:

  • How often do you participate in physical activities?
  • What's your favorite form of physical activity?
  • Do you have a device to track your activity?

I found out that the most common participation in physical activity was 2-4/week. I found out that walking/jogging/running was the most common form of physical activity. I found out the most people don’t use a device to track their activity.

With this information, I settled on doing a step tracker because it has the ability to track the user's physical activity with the amount of steps they’ve done but also has other factors to take in such as to check the light level and temperature outside and see if that would affect the user's walk. My project is written in Python in the IDE Thonny.
